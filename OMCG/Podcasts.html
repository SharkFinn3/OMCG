Podcasts

Episode 1: World's Youngest Professional Drummer - Julian Pavone

Hamza Islam starts the first episode of the podcast by briefly talking about himself and the vision he has for the podcast before his conversation with Julian Pavone. Julian Pavone is the World's Youngest Professional Drummer - a record he has held since the age of four. In addition, he has also received two patents for his inventions before the age of 10. His first came when he was 4 years old and his second came when he was 7 years old. In this conversation, Julian reflects on his incredible journey from the time he was born to where he is now and his predictions for the future. Not only does he hope to continue to amaze people in the future but also encourages other people to follow their passion and not being afraid to fail or care about what other people say about you.

Episode 2: Illinois State Representative - Nabeela Syed

Illinois State Representative Nabeela Syed is one of the youngest members of the Illinois General Assembly. Despite being 23 years old, she won her race on Election Day in November. In this episode, the newly elected Illinois State Representative discusses her campaign journey and what was going through her mind when winning the Democratic Primary and the Election Race. She also gives advice to other young people who want to make a difference in their communities despite being told to wait their turn or that they are not old enough.

Episode 3: Undergrad Student Body President @ OSU - Andrew Pierce

Andrew Pierce is the current Undergraduate Student Body President at The Ohio State University. In addition, he is the co-founder of the WORTH Foundation which aims to equip young people with the skills they need in order to create change in their communities. In this episode, Andrew talks to Hamza about what it is like to serve as an Undergraduate Student Body President and representing more than 53,000 undergraduate students as well as the importance of civics education in local communities. He also talks about how his parents have helped him become the person he is today and what is next for him after college.

Episode 4: CEO of The Conversationalist - Sophie Beren

Sophie Beren is the Founder and CEO of The Conversationalist - a non-partisan educational platform founded to empower and unify the world through conversations. During her time at Penn University, Sophie created TableTalk which allowed people from different conversations as well as The Conversationalist. In addition, Sophie has her how own talk show called the POVz in which she allows her guests to talk about their journey to how they became the person they are today. In this episode, Sophie talks about the importance of having difficult conversations, what it means to be vulnerable, and whether she would ever consider running for President of The United States in the future.
